**Call for Papers**
-------------------

**Ebenezer: A Journal of Public Theology & Public Witness**

**Rolling Submissions | Now Open**

**Ebenezer** invites submissions on a rolling basis for publication in **Ebenezer: A Journal of Public Theology & Public Witness**. We publish writing that brings careful scholarship into public life, and that offers clear, accountable witness in the face of urgent social, political, and moral questions.

### **Who may submit**

Submissions are open to authors who:

-   are **currently enrolled** in an **accredited graduate program**, **or**

-   **hold an accredited graduate degree** (MA, MDiv, MTS, ThM, PhD, DMin, JD, or equivalent)

We welcome work from scholars, clergy, educators, practitioners, and public writers working across traditions and disciplines.

### **What we publish**

We seek original work that is well-argued, well-sourced, and written for readers beyond a narrow specialty.

**Topics may include (but are not limited to):**

-   public theology and public ethics

-   religion, race, empire, and colonialism

-   democracy, nationalism, and political power

-   land, water, climate, and environmental justice

-   migration, borders, and belonging

-   gender, sexuality, and embodied life

-   media, technology, and digital public life

-   interreligious encounter and ecumenical practice

-   memory, violence, repair, and reconciliation

**Genres and formats:**

-   **Essays / Articles** (2,000--6,000 words)

-   **Public theology interventions** (1,200--2,500 words)

-   **Field reports / praxis essays** (1,500--3,500 words)

-   **Interviews / conversations** (2,000--5,000 words)

-   **Book reviews** (800--1,500 words)

-   **Teaching resources** (1,000--3,000 words; syllabus notes, assignments, annotated guides)

If your piece does not fit these categories, submit a short pitch.

### **What we value**

We give priority to submissions that are:

-   **public-facing** without losing scholarly care

-   **clear in prose** and strong in structure

-   **grounded in sources** and accountable to communities discussed

-   **ecumenical in heritage, interreligious in outlook, inclusive by design**

-   attentive to the realities of power, domination, and lived experience

### **Editorial review process**

All submissions receive editorial screening. Depending on the genre and the claim, a piece may move forward through:

-   **editorial review and revision**, and/or

-   **anonymous peer review**

We edit for clarity, argument, and public accessibility. We do not publish work that targets groups for harm or trades in misinformation.

### **Submission guidelines**

Please submit:

1.  **Manuscript** (Word or Google Doc link), anonymized if possible

2.  **Abstract** (150--200 words)

3.  **Author bio** (60--100 words)

4.  **Academic status** (current graduate program and institution, or degree held and granting institution)

5.  **5 keywords**

6.  Any relevant **conflicts of interest** or community relationships tied to the piece

Use a consistent citation style (Chicago, MLA, APA, or Turabian are all acceptable). If accepted, we may ask you to conform to house style.

### **How to submit**

Submissions are **open now** and accepted on a **rolling basis**. Send your materials through the journal's submission portal or contact email listed on the Ebenezer website.

**Ebenezer** publishes work meant to be read, taught, shared, and put to use. We invite you to write with us.
